🌳 Exam Decision Tree — 4 Questions
Q1
Particle or rigid body?
All forces at one point → PARTICLE (ΣFx, ΣFy only) Object has size, forces at different points → RIGID BODY (+ΣM)
Q2
Reactions or internal forces?
Support reactions → FBD of whole object → 3 equations Internal forces (Ch 7) → find reactions FIRST, then cut beam
Q3
Scalar or cross product for moments?
Easy perpendicular distance → SCALAR (M = F × d) Messy angles / 3D → CROSS PRODUCT (M = r × F)
Q4
Which moment point?
Pick where most unknowns act → they vanish (zero moment arm) Pin at A? → ΣM_A kills Ax and Ay in one shot
🔻 Decomposition Tree — Backward Chaining

Start from the ANSWER, expand unknowns downward until you hit givens, then solve upward.

FIND: Reaction B_y │ ├── Equation: ΣM_A = 0 (eliminates Ax, Ay) │ │ │ ├── B_y × distance_AB ← solve for this │ ├── −P × distance_AP ← P is given, d is geometry │ └── −W × distance_AW ← W is given or "neglect" │ └── Result: B_y = (P·d_AP + W·d_AW) / d_AB Then back-substitute: FIND: A_y └── ΣF_y = 0 → A_y = P + W − B_y ← all known now FIND: A_x └── ΣF_x = 0 → A_x = ... ← from horizontal forces
⚡ 7 Equations — 3 Bosses, 4 Workers
Boss Equations (set = 0)
BOSS 1 ΣFx = 0 Left–right forces cancel
BOSS 2 ΣFy = 0 Up–down forces cancel
BOSS 3 ΣM = 0 Rotation cancels (CCW = +)
Worker Equations (compute ingredients)
→ Fx Fx = F cos θ Feeds into Boss 1
→ Fy Fy = F sin θ Feeds into Boss 2
→ M scalar M = F × d Feeds into Boss 3 (easy geometry)
→ M vector M = r × F Feeds into Boss 3 (messy angles / 3D)

⚠️ Common Traps
⚠️
Negative answer ≠ mistake. It means you guessed the arrow direction wrong. Magnitude is correct — just flip the arrow.
⚠️
Gravity default: always draw it unless the problem says "neglect weight" or "weightless." When in doubt, include it.
⚠️
Perpendicular distance, not just any distance. Extend force into a line → drop a 90° line from the point to that line. Or: break into components and use simple horizontal/vertical distances.
⚠️
Fixed support has 3 unknowns, not 2. Don't forget the moment reaction at a wall/cement connection.
⚠️
Wrong angle reference. θ from horizontal → cos for x, sin for y. θ from vertical → sin for x, cos for y. Always check which axis θ is measured from.
⚠️
Professor tests BOTH moment methods. Expect: (a) scalar F×d AND (b) r × F cross product for the SAME problem. Practice both.
