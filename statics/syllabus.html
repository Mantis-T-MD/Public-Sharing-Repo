Course Description

Resolution and composition of force systems and equilibrium of force systems acting on structures and mechanisms. Distributed forces. Friction. Moments of inertia. (4 Units)

Course Learning Outcomes (CLO)

  1. Calculate the resultant force due to a system of forces.
  2. Calculate moments about points or lines for 2D or 3D systems.
  3. Calculate external reactions for statically-determinate, rigid bodies in 2D and 3D equilibrium.
  4. Draw Free Body Diagrams for rigid bodies in 2D and 3D equilibrium.
  5. Calculate internal member forces in trusses, frames, cables, machines, and beams using equations of static equilibrium.
  6. Develop shear and moment equations for beams.
  7. Solve static dry friction problems.
  8. Determine the location of centroids (2D) and centers of gravity (3D) for various shapes.
  9. Determine the 2nd Moment of Area (Moment of Inertia) of various figures.
  10. Present problem solutions in a clear, logical manner with free-body diagrams and a clearly documented solution strategy.
  11. Identify the importance of accurately determining system loading and support reactions to prevent catastrophic system failure by doing a real-life group project at the campus. The team will measure a real structure and analyze it as a project. Students will understand how to run a project with professional approach, learn about professional licensure by talking to engineers, apply statics knowledge to analyze real structures, use techniques/skills/interdisciplinary and modern engineering tools, identify/formulate/solve engineering problems, understand systems within realistic constraints (project management, economic, environmental, social, political, ethical, health and safety, manufacturability, sustainability), and support community-based learning by interacting with campus authorities for measurements.
  12. Present the project in a public speech — discuss findings from the real project, compare with course theories, and demonstrate effective communication.

Faculty are required to report all infractions to the Office of Student Life. Copying homework/coursework from another student or an online source is cheating.
